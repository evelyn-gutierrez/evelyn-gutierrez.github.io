Biography

Ph.D. candidate in Biomedical Engineering at PUCP at Lima, Peru. My thesis focuses on developing and validating metrics for monitoring wounds using color and thermal information obtained with smartphones and portable devices.

Also, I am Lecturer at the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru and Co-Organizer of RLadies Lima, a worldwide organization that promotes the participation of women and minorities in programming with the statistical software R.

Combined thermal and color 3D model for wound evaluation from handheld devices

A methodology is proposed for multimodal wound monitoring in the hospital environment with a simple hand-held shooting protocol. The methodology we present is able to generate color and thermal 3D models by using portable devices; and was used efficiently with 5 patients on wounds of various sizes and types.
