Biography

PhD student in Informatics at the Université d’Orléans (France) in cotutelle with the Pontificia Universidad Católica del Perú (PUCP). My thesis is focused on the use of 3D models and thermography for the visualization and development of metrics in chronic wound monitoring.

Part-time lecturer at PUCP with an interest in the development of statistical thinking.

Professional experience in consulting, banking and retail. In particular, with psychometric data analysis and modeling, geographic information (GIS), structured and unstructured data. Proficient in prediction and evaluation of machine learning models and classical statistical models, statistical inference and time series analysis.

Expert in programming and reproducibility with R, Python, Stata, and SQL.

Languages: Spanish (Native), English (Advanced) and French (Upper Intermediate)

Multimodal and Multiview Wound Monitoring with Mobile Devices
In this study, we propose the use of commercial devices, such as mobile devices and portable thermography, to integrate information from different wavelengths onto the surface of a 3D model. Thermal and color 3D models were successfully created for six patients with multiple views from a low-cost commercial device.

Combined thermal and color 3D model for wound evaluation from handheld devices
A methodology is proposed for multimodal wound monitoring in the hospital environment with a simple hand-held shooting protocol. The methodology we present is able to generate color and thermal 3D models by using portable devices; and was used efficiently with 5 patients on wounds of various sizes and types.
